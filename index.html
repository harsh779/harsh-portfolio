I went directly to the live timing source instead of building on top of public wrappers. That meant handling live topics, differential patch updates, binary decompression, state modeling, reconnect logic, and disk persistence myself.

03
Consumer Intelligence · Live in Production

YouTube
Intelligence

A production tool that closes the biggest blind spot in social listening: the influence of YouTube creators on consumer perception and buying behaviour — and the audience reaction that follows.

Most social listening tools monitor Twitter, Reddit, and news. None of them treat YouTubers as what they actually are: distributed, domain-expert consumer panels with high-trust, self-selected audiences. This tool does — and goes further by layering in comment-level audience reaction as a second, real-time signal.

Why it matters: the $5B+ social listening industry is structurally blind to YouTube creator influence. A 15-minute creator teardown shifts purchase intent more than 50 tweets. Brands using traditional tools are missing one of the most commercially significant perception signals available.

What I Solved

This is not a YouTube wrapper. The insight was recognising that creator-led perception sits entirely outside what social listening tools measure — and that the comment section is an underutilised signal layer that turns a creator's opinion into a measurable demand signal.

I designed the full pipeline: query intake with regional context → YouTube discovery → transcript extraction → aspect-based sentiment scoring → comment synthesis → persisted executive report. The architecture uses BullMQ and Redis for async processing at scale, with a fallback sample data mode so the app stays live even if the database is unavailable.

The gap this closes: brands spend heavily on social listening, but YouTubers — who drive more purchase intent than any text-based social platform in categories like consumer tech, beauty, and CPG — are invisible in that data. This doesn't miss them.
